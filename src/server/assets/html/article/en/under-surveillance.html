Introduction

Under Surveillance is the game Adrián Navarro Pérez and I developed for our Master's Thesis.

In the game, the player takes control of a mysterious robot who can use electricity and water to solve a set of puzzles while unveiling the hidden enigma behind an uncharted dystopian world.

Development

Under Surveillance took approximately six months to develop (the time we had available for our thesis). The development time can be split in six different phases.

Phase 1: Prototyping

In this phase we focused on coming up with ideas and concretising the game we wanted to develop. We thought that a good starting point would be to write on paper our ideas in terms of mechanics, narrative, levels, etc.

These are some of the sketches we made.

Phase 2: Designing the game

As a part of our thesis we developed a specific game design methodology called the PEP framework. We used this methodology to design the game.

In this phase we essentially followed a formal process we had already created before to design the game mechanics, the environment and the interactions based on the ideas brainstormed in the first phase.

Phase 3: First version of the game

In this phase we started to develop the game itself using Unity. As a the first version it was, our focus was primarily on creating a basic prototype of the game to test whether or not the ideas we had in our minds made sense and felt meaningful to play when implemented.

These is a screenshot of this version of the game.

Despite all the effort we put in this phase, we were self-critical enough to realise that the development was not going in the appropriate direction. We thought that this was mainly due to the level-design philosophy.

Phase 4: Second version of the game

Since in the previous phase we thought that our level-design was the root cause of all the problems present in the game, we discarded the previous level and started a new one from scratch.

In particular, we focused on providing a more meaningful experience by turning Under Surveillance into puzzle-solving game instead of a simple point-and-click.

These are some screenshots of the game with this new level.

However, while this change brought some improvements to the game, we felt that there were some aspects that still were not clicking for us.

Phase 5: Final version of the game

Finally, we realised that our main mistake was having a scope that was too broad.

From this point, we shifted our development philosophy radically into simplicity and minimalism. Simpler games are easier to polish in every sense. In this regard, we focused on creating a polished experience instead of adding complexity, both in terms of the graphics and the gameplay.

With this philosophy in mind, we managed to develop the final version of the game.
